About


Data + Design + Democracy = Healthy Places


I am a third-year PhD student in Urbanism at Georgia Institute of Technology with a secondary degree in Statistics. My research interest is built upon what I have been consistently motivated throughout the way: to achieve a healthy urban environment through data empowerment, urban design interventions, and democratic planning processes.


Leveraging both data-driven methods and design-oriented thinking, I work with Prof. Ellen Dunham-Jones on topics across food access, retrofitting suburbia, and so on. I also work as a research assistant in the Just Housing Lab directed by Dr. Elora Raymond on evictions and housing crisis during COVID-19. In 2021, I receive a generous fellowship from Brook Bryers Institute for Sustainable Systems to look into urgent urban sustainability issues with a multidisciplinary team.


Prior to entering the doctorate program, I graduated from Tongji University with a B.S. and M.S. in Urban Planning.

Research


My research focuses on the interplay between health and the built environment, and seeks to answer questions such as "what makes some places healthier then others", and "which factors lead to health injustice among different places".


Under that overarching topic, most of my work is anchored in Food Access, COVID-19, and how they relate to our physical and conceived environment. Using data-driven methods, I rely heavily on quantitative evidence to provide a retractable interpretation on differences in socio-economic outcomes.


Besides that, I also support research in the topic of housing insecurity, sustainability, and urban design.

Research Report

Large corporate buyers of residential rental housing during the COVID-19 pandemic

In this research brief, we describe purchases of multifamily and single family rentals in the year before and after the emergence of the COVID19 pandemic. We rely on ZTRAX, Zillow’s database linking deeded transactions with tax assessors’ data, to understand purchasing patterns rental residential real estate during the pandemic.

Elora Raymond, Yilun Zha, Ethan Knight-Scott and Leah Cabrera
Research Paper

Geospatial Modeling of Food Access and Nutrient-related Disease Rate in Atlanta Metro Area

Using Yelp businesses and USDA SNAP retailer data, this study proposes a multidimensional and spatiotemporally dynamic approach to measuring food access in metropolitan Atlanta from the perspective of Abundance, Diversity, and Healthiness.

Yilun Zha

Teaching


I enjoy the teaching atmosphere and conversations with students. Although my main task assigned is not to lecture, I communicate, support, and inspire students through conversations, assignment feedbacks, and gradings. More importantly, I also have the opportunity to exchange ideas and gain insights into issues from new perspectives.

Since 2019, I have served as the teaching assistant for the following graduate-level courses:

  • (2019 Fall, 2020 Fall) ARCH-6151 Theories of Urban Design
  • (2020 Spring, 2021 Spring) ARCH-7151 History of Urban Form
  • (2021 Spring) ARCH-6100 Retrofitting Suburbia

Volunteer

Planning and urban design should not only be a profession, but a shared public service. With that idea in mind, I take every opportunities possible since undergrad to support, inform, and educate local communities.

From 2012-2014, serving as the deputy chair of Public Relations in CAUP Student Union, I with my colleagues organized more than 10 episodes of our signature "City Walking" event, the purpose of which is to invite academics and professionals to guide a free-to-public walk around the city and provide professional insights on urbanism, architecture, and so on. (The food is good as well!)

In 2016, Tracy Zhang and I served as volunteer designers to help Shiquan Community in Shanghai with a micro renewal project for street bike-fixing booth on the sidewalk of Shiquan Rd.

In 2020, a group of students from Georgia Tech including me helped Atlanta Community Food Bank to build a new warmhouse on a community garden in South Atlanta.
